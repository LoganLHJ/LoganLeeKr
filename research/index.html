Research Overview

My research focuses on generating biological insight from large-scale genomic and multi-omics datasets through the integration of data engineering, computational biology, and biomedical analytics. I specialize in developing robust, scalable pipelines for the processing, harmonization, and interpretation of population-scale omics data, with an emphasis on enabling reproducible and clinically relevant analyses. Through my work on nationwide whole-genome sequencing (WGS) initiatives in the Korean population, I have gained deep experience in variant discovery, quality refinement, cohort-level integration, and downstream functional interpretation of genomic data.

A central aim of my research is to bridge raw, high-dimensional biological data with domain-informed models that elucidate molecular mechanisms and disease pathways. To accomplish this, I leverage multi-omics integration approaches that combine genomic, transcriptomic, epigenomic, and proteomic signals, enabling the construction of cross-layer frameworks that capture the complexity of human biology. I am particularly interested in approaches that unify statistical genomics with computational modeling to enhance interpretability and translate molecular variation into meaningful biological hypotheses.

In parallel, I seek to expand my work beyond genomic analytics toward protein-level modeling and computational frameworks with applications in therapeutic discovery. Protein structure, functional annotation, and molecular interaction modeling offer critical opportunities to connect population-level variation with mechanistic consequences at the molecular scale. By integrating sequence-based models, structure-informed prediction methods, and AI-driven analytical tools, my long-term goal is to establish data-driven pipelines that link genetic variation to protein behavior and disease phenotypes.

Ultimately, my research aims to create end-to-end computational systems that support precision medicine, from raw data engineering to downstream biological interpretation and therapeutic innovation. By combining scalable computational infrastructure, rigorous statistical methods, and mechanistic biological reasoning, I aim to contribute to a framework where population-scale omics data can inform the development of targeted diagnostics, personalized treatment strategies, and novel therapeutic approaches.
