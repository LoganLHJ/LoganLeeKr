Research Overview

My research centers on extracting biological insight from large-scale genomic and multi-omics datasets by integrating data engineering, computational biology, and biomedical analytics. I develop robust, scalable pipelines for processing and harmonizing population-scale omics data, with a focus on reproducibility and clinical relevance. Through nationwide whole-genome sequencing (WGS) efforts in the Korean population, I have gained extensive experience in variant discovery, quality refinement, cohort integration, and downstream functional interpretation.

A core goal of my work is to connect high-dimensional biological data with domain-informed models that reveal molecular mechanisms and disease pathways. I apply multi-omics integration strategies that combine genomic, transcriptomic, epigenomic, and proteomic information to build cross-layer frameworks that better capture biological complexity. I am particularly interested in unifying statistical genomics with computational modeling to enhance interpretability and generate meaningful biological hypotheses.

In parallel, I aim to extend my work toward protein-level modeling and computational approaches for therapeutic discovery. Protein structure, functional annotation, and interaction modeling provide key opportunities to link population-level variation with molecular consequences. By combining sequence-based models, structure-informed prediction, and AI-driven analytical tools, my long-term objective is to build pipelines that connect genetic variation to protein behavior and disease phenotypes.

Ultimately, my research seeks to develop end-to-end computational systems that advance precision medicine—from raw data engineering to biological interpretation and therapeutic innovation—supporting the development of targeted diagnostics and personalized treatment strategies.
