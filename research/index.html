Research Overview

My research focuses on deriving biological insight from large-scale genomic and multi-omics datasets through scalable data engineering and computational biology. I build reproducible pipelines for population-scale omics analysis, with experience spanning nationwide WGS projects, variant discovery, cohort-level harmonization, and functional interpretation.

I aim to connect high-dimensional biological data with models that reveal molecular mechanisms and disease pathways. To achieve this, I employ multi-omics integration and combine statistical genomics with machine learning and deep learning approaches to extract interpretable biological signals across genomic, transcriptomic, epigenomic, and proteomic layers.

My work also extends to protein-level modeling, including structure-informed prediction and variant effect analysis, to link genetic variation to protein function and disease phenotypes. Ultimately, I seek to develop end-to-end computational frameworks that advance precision medicine and inform data-driven therapeutic development.

Pipelines & Tools I Develop

  • Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS), RNA-seq, and ChIP-seq Analysis Pipelines
    End-to-end workflows for high-throughput sequencing data processing, quality control, alignment, quantification, and downstream interpretation.
  • Structural Variome Pipeline
    An automated framework that generates wild-type and mutant protein sequences, predicts 3D structures (AlphaFold2), and computes structure-based feature scores to assess the functional impact of genetic variants.
  • GTEx Data Parsing Toolkit
    Custom modules for efficient extraction, normalization, and integration of GTEx expression and genotype datasets to support large-scale transcriptomic and genetic analyses.
